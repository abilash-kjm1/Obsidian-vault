## On-Site SOC  / In-House SOC

![](Attachments/Pasted%20image%2020260624223328.png)

An **Onsite SOC** (also known as an On-Premises SOC) is a physical, dedicated security operations center located directly inside an organization's own building or facility.

Instead of outsourcing security to a third-party company or hosting it entirely in the cloud, the organization builds, owns, and operates everything locally.

Here is a quick breakdown of what makes it unique in easy terms:

### Key Features

- **Physical Infrastructure:** A dedicated room filled with screens, dashboards, and secure hardware located inside the company's building.
    
- **Internal Staff:** The Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 analysts, as well as the SOC Manager, are full-time employees of the company working on-location.
    
- **Direct Control:** The organization has 100% control over its security data, tools, and the procedures its team follows.
    

### Main Advantages

- **Maximum Data Privacy:** Because everything stays inside the company's physical walls, it is ideal for highly regulated industries (like banks, government agencies, or defense contractors) that cannot risk sending sensitive data over the internet to a third party.
    
- **Faster Local Collaboration:** Being in the same building means the SOC team can coordinate instantly with internal IT staff and network teams during a critical incident.
    

### Main Challenges

- **Very High Cost:** Building an onsite facility requires a massive budget for secure room construction, expensive hardware/software licenses, and hiring a full 24/7 internal team.
    
- **Maintenance Burden:** The organization is entirely responsible for managing, upgrading, and fixing the security technologies and keeping the physical space running 24/7.

## Outsourced SOC /MSSP -  [ Managed Service Security Provider]

![](Attachments/Pasted%20image%2020260624224324.png)
### **Outsourced SOC / MSSP (Managed Security Service Provider)**

- **What it is:** Hiring an external, third-party cybersecurity company to monitor your network and handle threats instead of doing it yourself.
    

### **Key Features**

- **External Team:** Analysts work for the MSSP, not your company, and monitor your network remotely from their own office.
    
- **Shared Infrastructure:** The vendor uses their own software and dashboards to watch multiple clients at once.
    
- **Remote Setup:** You install their monitoring tools on your systems, and they immediately start tracking your data.
    

### **Main Advantages**

- **Cost-Effective:** No expensive hardware to buy or full-time 24/7 salaries to pay—you just pay a subscription fee.
    
- **Instant 24/7 Protection:** You get immediate access to an expert security team without spending months hiring or training people.
    
- **Less Noise for IT:** The MSSP filters out false alarms and only alerts your internal team when a threat is 100% real.
    

### **Main Challenges**

- **Less Control:** You must rely on the vendor's specific tools, rules, and standard procedures.
    
- **Data Sharing:** Sensitive network logs must be sent outside your company walls to a third party.
    
- **Divided Attention:** Because the MSSP manages many companies at once, you aren't their only priority.

## Global SOC
![](Attachments/Pasted%20image%2020260624225006.png)

### **Global SOC**

- **What it is:** A centralized security model where a large organization operates multiple interconnected SOC facilities located across different countries or continents.
    
- **Purpose:** It allows a company to maintain seamless security monitoring across various global offices, regions, and time zones under a unified strategy.
    

### **Follow-the-Sun Approach**

- **What it is:** A workflow strategy where security monitoring and incident response duties are handed off to a SOC team in a different time zone as their workday begins.
    
- **How it works:** When the workday ends for the team in North America, they hand over active investigations and monitoring responsibilities to a team starting their morning in Asia or Europe.
    

### **Key Benefits of Follow-the-Sun**

- **True 24/7 Coverage Without Night Shifts:** Instead of forcing a local team to work exhausting overnight shifts, the work is always handled by awake, alert analysts working during their normal daytime hours.
    
- **Faster Response Times:** Active threat investigations don't stall when a local team goes home; the next global team picks up the file immediately, keeping remediation moving continuously.
    
- **Local Language & Regional Expertise:** Having global teams ensures that local language barriers, regional threat intelligence, and specific geographic compliance regulations are handled by experts in those areas.
