## On-Site SOC  / In-House SOC

![](Attachments/Pasted%20image%2020260624223328.png)

An **Onsite SOC** (also known as an On-Premises SOC) is a physical, dedicated security operations center located directly inside an organization's own building or facility.

Instead of outsourcing security to a third-party company or hosting it entirely in the cloud, the organization builds, owns, and operates everything locally.

Here is a quick breakdown of what makes it unique in easy terms:

### Key Features

- **Physical Infrastructure:** A dedicated room filled with screens, dashboards, and secure hardware located inside the company's building.
    
- **Internal Staff:** The Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 analysts, as well as the SOC Manager, are full-time employees of the company working on-location.
    
- **Direct Control:** The organization has 100% control over its security data, tools, and the procedures its team follows.
    

### Main Advantages

- **Maximum Data Privacy:** Because everything stays inside the company's physical walls, it is ideal for highly regulated industries (like banks, government agencies, or defense contractors) that cannot risk sending sensitive data over the internet to a third party.
    
- **Faster Local Collaboration:** Being in the same building means the SOC team can coordinate instantly with internal IT staff and network teams during a critical incident.
    

### Main Challenges

- **Very High Cost:** Building an onsite facility requires a massive budget for secure room construction, expensive hardware/software licenses, and hiring a full 24/7 internal team.
    
- **Maintenance Burden:** The organization is entirely responsible for managing, upgrading, and fixing the security technologies and keeping the physical space running 24/7.
