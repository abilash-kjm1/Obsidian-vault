
















## NIST CSF 2.0 

As promised, here are your concise, high-yield study notes written in simple English to help you absolute ace any interview questions on this topic!

### 1. What is the NIST CSF?

It is a voluntary framework (a systematic playbook) designed to help organizations of any size manage, reduce, and communicate cybersecurity risks clearly.

### 2. The 6 Core Functions (The Pillars)

- **GOVERN (GV):** Sets the company's security strategy, rules, and roles. _(The brain)_
    
- **IDENTIFY (ID):** Inventories assets (physical devices, software, data) to know what needs protection. _(The inventory)_
    
- **PROTECT (PR):** Secures the environment to limit or contain cyber impact (Firewalls, patching, training). _(The shield)_
    
- **DETECT (DE):** Finds anomalous security events quickly via continuous monitoring (SIEM, EDR logs). _(The watchtower — **SOC Domain**)_
    
- **RESPOND (RS):** Takes immediate action once a threat is detected to minimize damage (Isolation, blocking). _(The fire brigade — **SOC Domain**)_
    
- **RECOVER (RC):** Restores any capabilities or services that were impaired by an attack. _(The cleanup crew)_
    

### 3. Key Vocabulary for a SOC Interview

- **SIEM (Security Information & Event Management):** Your central dashboard that collects and correlates logs across the network to power the **Detect** function.
    
- **EDR (Endpoint Detection & Response):** Tool installed on devices (like laptops) to monitor behaviors and execute **Respond** actions like isolating a host from the network.
    
- **True Positive vs. False Positive:** A True Positive is a real, legitimate alarm (like our scenario). A False Positive is a false alarm.
